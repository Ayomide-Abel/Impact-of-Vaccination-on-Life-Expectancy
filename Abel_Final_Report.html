### Introduction
Vaccination programs have been pivotal in improving global health by reducing preventable mortality rates. This study 
examines how vaccination rates, particularly for vaccines such as MCV1 (measles) and DTP3 (diphtheria, tetanus, 
pertussis), correlate with life expectancy. By analyzing global data, we aim to quantify the broader impact of 
immunization on health outcomes.

**Research Question**:
- How do vaccination rates impact life expectancy across countries?

**Hypotheses**:
1. **Null Hypothesis (H)**: Vaccination coverage does not significantly impact life expectancy.
2. **Alternative Hypothesis (H)**: Vaccination coverage significantly improves life expectancy, particularly for vaccines 
   like MCV1 and DTP3.

# Import necessary libraries
import pandas as pd
import numpy as np
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns

# Load datasets
vaccination_data = pd.read_csv('global-vaccination-coverage/global-vaccination-coverage.csv')
life_expectancy_data = pd.read_csv('life-expectancy/life-expectancy.csv')

# Rename columns for clarity
life_expectancy_data.rename(columns={
    'Entity': 'Country', 
    'Period life expectancy at birth - Sex: all - Age: 0': 'Life Expectancy'
}, inplace=True)
vaccination_data.rename(columns={'Entity': 'Country'}, inplace=True)

# Select relevant columns for vaccination data
vaccination_columns = ['Country', 'Year', 
                       'BCG (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'HepB3 (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'Hib3 (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'IPV1 (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'MCV1 (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'PCV3 (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'Pol3 (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'RCV1 (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'RotaC (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'YFV (% of one-year-olds immunized)', 
                       'DTP3 (% of one-year-olds immunized)']
vaccination_data = vaccination_data[vaccination_columns]

# Select relevant columns for life expectancy data
life_expectancy_data = life_expectancy_data[['Country', 'Year', 'Life Expectancy']]

# Merge datasets on Country and Year
merged_data = pd.merge(vaccination_data, life_expectancy_data, on=['Country', 'Year'])

# Drop missing values for consistency
merged_data.dropna(inplace=True)

Discussion

The analysis reveals a significant positive relationship between vaccination coverage and life expectancy, with MCV1 showing the strongest association. This result aligns with global health policies emphasizing measles vaccination to reduce mortality. However, limitations include potential confounding factors such as socioeconomic status, healthcare access, and geographic disparities. Future research should incorporate these variables to provide a more holistic understanding.

Conclusion

This study highlights the critical role of vaccination programs in enhancing global health outcomes. Vaccines like MCV1 and DTP3 significantly contribute to increased life expectancy, emphasizing the need for sustained immunization efforts. Policymakers should prioritize expanding vaccine coverage to maximize public health benefits.
